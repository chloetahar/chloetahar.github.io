Research interests

  • Context-sensitivity of the interpretation of modals

    Since the seminal works of Kratzer (1977, 1981), it is generally assumed that the truth-conditions of modals are tied up to the extra-linguistic context. Focusing on the actual use of modals as represented in corpus, I show that the context may supply the prospective interpretation of non-veridical modals (focusing on ‘fear’ and ‘before’) with teleological or stereotypical orderings, yielding specific discourse functions.

    • Tahar, Chloe (2021). Apprehensive and frustrative uses of ‘before’. Semantic and Linguistic Theory 31. [Preprint] [Lightning Talk]

    • Tahar, Chloe (2021). Craindre (‘fear’) and expletive negation. Romance Languages and Linguistic Theory 2018. John Benjamins. [Preprint]

  • Grammaticalization

    The gradualness of the diachronic change from parataxis to hypotaxis in systems of complementation is an open question. What are the intermediary stages? In these joint works with Alda Mari, we tackle the syntactic and semantic mechanisms at play in the reanalysis of prohibitive negation as expletive, by focusing on the rise of an overt hypotactic system of complementation during the transition from Latin to French

    • Tahar, Chloe and Mari, Alda (manuscript, IJN). Turning expletive: from embedded speech-acts to embedded propositions.

    • Tahar, Chloe (2021). Expletive negation: from embedded speech-acts to embedded propositions. Negation@DGfS: Empirical Approaches to Canonical and Non-Canonical Uses of Negation Workshop at the 43rd Annual Meeting of the German Linguistics Society (DGfS), Freiburg. [Slides]

    • Mari, Alda and Tahar, Chloe (2020). Negative priorities : evidence from prohibitive and expletive negation. Proceedings of Sinn und Bedeuntung 24(2): 56-71. [Publication]
