Chloe's homepage

Hello! my name is Chloe Tahar and thanks for reading. I’m currently finishing my dissertation: Expletive Negation: from imperatives to connectives. A formal diachronic approach, under the supervision of Alda Mari at the Institut Jean Nicod (ENS). You can check my CV here.

My main research interests are in formal semantics and its interface with pragmatics, with a specific interest for the semantics of clause-types, attitude verbs, as well as connectives and for diagnosing projective content. Empirically, I’m exploring (diachronic) corpus investigation methods, as well as research methods from historical and comparative linguistics (with a focus on Indo-European languages).

Get in touch with me by e-mail at chloe.tahar@gmail.com

Research interests

  • Contextual influences on the interpretation of modals

    Since the seminal works of Kratzer (1977, 1981), it is generally assumed that the truth-conditions of modals are tied up to the extra-linguistic context. Focusing on the actual use of modals as represented in corpus, I show that the context may supply the prospective interpretation of non-veridical modals (focusing on ‘fear’ and ‘before’) with teleological or stereotypical orderings, yielding specific discourse functions.

    • Tahar, Chloe (2021). Apprehensive and frustrative uses of ‘before’. Semantic and Linguistic Theory 31. [Preprint]

    • Tahar, Chloe (2021). Craindre (‘fear’) and expletive negation. Romance Languages and Linguistic Theory 2018. John Benjamins. [Preprint]

  • Grammaticalization

    • Tahar, Chloe and Mari, Alda (manuscript, IJN). Turning expletive: from embedded speech-acts to embedded propositions.

    • Mari, Alda and Tahar, Chloe (2020). Negative priorities : evidence from prohibitive and expletive negation. Proceedings of Sinn und Bedeuntung 24(2): 56-71. [Publication]
